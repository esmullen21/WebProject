Victoria Sponge Recipe

To:  




  • Ingredients

  • Knob of butter, melted
  • 8 oz butter, softened
  • 8 oz sugar
  • 4 large eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 8 oz all-purpose flour, sifted
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder

  • For the Filling:
  • Strawberry jam
  • 240 ml heavy whipping cream, lightly whipped

  • To serve:
  • Powdered sugar, for dusting

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F.
    Gently heat the knob of butter in a pan and brush two 8 inch cake tins with the melted butter.
    Line the bottom of the two cake tins with a circle of greaseproof paper.
    2.  
  2. In a large bowl, cream together the butter and sugar until pale and creamy, using an electric whisk or a wooden spoon.
    Beat well to ensure you get lots of air into the mixture.
    Beat in the eggs one at a time. You want to avoid the mixture curdling, so that it stays airy. If it does curdle, add a tbsp of flour.
    3.  
  3. Sift together the flour, baking powder and salt.
    Sift together the flour, baking powder and salt.
    Fold the flour mixture into the wet ingredients using a large metal spoon (this will cut into the mixture better than a wooden spoon).
    Be careful not to over-mix it. Pour the mixture equally between the two cake tins and level.
    4.  
  4. Place in the oven and bake for about 20-25 minutes, or until the cakes are well-risen, spring back when pressed gently with a finger and a skewer inserted into the middle comes out clean.
    Remove from the oven and set aside for 5 minutes, then remove from the tin and peel off the paper.
    Place onto a wire rack and let cool completely, about an hour.
    5.  
  5. Sandwich the cakes together with the jam and whipped cream.
    Dust with powdered sugar and serve.
